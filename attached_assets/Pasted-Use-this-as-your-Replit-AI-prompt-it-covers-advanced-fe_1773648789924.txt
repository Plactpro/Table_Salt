***

Build fully functional advanced modules for the **Table Salt** restaurant management web application, implementing the following features end‑to‑end:

Focus only on these 9 advanced feature areas (integrated with the existing POS, billing, CRM, outlets, and settings modules):

1. Advanced Inventory & Recipe Costing  
2. Kitchen Display System (KDS)  
3. Online Ordering & Aggregator Hub  
4. Loyalty, Digital Wallet & Gift Cards  
5. Multi‑Outlet & Franchise Console  
6. Supplier, Procurement & Cost Control  
7. BI Dashboards & Forecasting  
8. Advanced Table & Queue Management  
9. Workforce & Labour Cost Module  

Use clean, modular JavaScript (or TypeScript if you prefer), HTML, and CSS. Keep everything in English. You may simulate data with JSON and localStorage; design the code so it can later be wired to real APIs.

***

## 1. Advanced Inventory & Recipe Costing

Implement a complete inventory system that tracks ingredients, stock movements, and recipe‑level costing:

- Data models:
  - Ingredients: id, name, category, base unit, unit conversions (kg ↔ g, litre ↔ ml, piece), cost per base unit, current stock, reorder level.
  - Recipes (dishes): id, name, outlet availability, list of ingredients with quantity and unit, yield/servings, theoretical cost per serving.
  - Stock transactions: type (purchase, adjustment, wastage, return, stock‑take), qty, cost, user, timestamp.
- Features:
  - Auto‑deplete stock when a dish is sold in POS (based on its recipe).
  - Manual stock‑take screen with variance calculation (expected vs counted).
  - Food cost % report: show ideal consumption vs actual, variance by ingredient and by dish.
  - Low‑stock alerts and reorder suggestions based on par levels and lead time.
- UI:
  - Inventory dashboard: cards for current stock value, stock at risk (near expiry), and top 10 moving items.
  - Recipe editor: pick ingredients from inventory, define quantities, auto‑calculate plate cost and margin using current selling price.
- Make all unit, cost, and quantity calculations robust and reusable via helper functions.

***

## 2. Kitchen Display System (KDS)

Build a KDS module that receives orders from POS and displays them in real time for different kitchen stations:

- Data:
  - KOT (Kitchen Order Ticket): id, order id, table, items, modifiers, course (starter/main/dessert), timestamp, station (main, grill, fryer, bar, pastry), status (new, in_progress, ready, served).
- Features:
  - Station views: each station sees only relevant items (e.g., “Grill” sees only grill items).
  - Status transitions with buttons: “Start”, “Ready”, “Recall”.
  - Color coding by elapsed time (green <5 min, orange 5–10, red >10).
  - Integration with POS: when KDS marks an item “Ready”, POS and table views update.
- UI:
  - Full‑screen KDS layout with large cards per order, grouped by table or ticket.
  - Sound/visual alert for new tickets and late tickets.
- Support offline‑friendly behavior using localStorage queues.

***

## 3. Online Ordering & Aggregator Hub

Create an omni‑channel orders module that consolidates internal online orders and third‑party delivery platforms:

- Order channels:
  - Direct web/QR orders (in‑house).
  - Aggregators: abstract integration points for Swiggy/Zomato/UberEats/etc. (mock with JSON feeds).
- Features:
  - “Orders Hub” screen listing all live orders with filters by channel, outlet, status.
  - Auto‑route orders to KDS and POS for billing and inventory deduction.
  - Menu sync concept: a central “Online Menu” mapping internal dishes to each aggregator’s item id and price (account for per‑channel pricing and availability).
- Provide an interface to:
  - Toggle channels per outlet.
  - Set channel‑specific prep times and packaging fees.
- Use a clean adapter pattern for aggregators so adding a new platform is trivial.

***

## 4. Loyalty, Digital Wallet & Gift Cards

Implement a complete loyalty and stored‑value system connected to CRM and POS:

- Data models:
  - Loyalty account: customer id, points balance, tier (Silver/Gold/Platinum), join date.
  - Transactions: earn/redeem events tied to bills, with multipliers by tier.
  - Wallet: stored value balance, top‑ups, redemptions, expiry rules.
  - Gift cards: card id, initial value, current balance, purchase and expiry dates, purchaser vs recipient.
- Rules:
  - Points earned as a configurable percentage of net spend after discounts and before tax.
  - Tier thresholds and automatic upgrades/downgrades.
- POS integration:
  - Search customer by phone/email, attach loyalty/wallet to the bill.
  - Apply points redemption or wallet funds as a payment method.
  - Show post‑transaction balances on the receipt.
- UI:
  - “Loyalty & Wallet” section inside CRM: per‑customer view of history, balances, and tier progress bars.
  - Gift card management: issue, top‑up, deactivate, view usage.

## 5. Multi‑Outlet & Franchise Console

Add a head‑office console for groups with multiple outlets and franchisees:

- Hierarchy:
  - Organization → Regions → Outlets; franchise vs company‑owned flag.
- Features:
  - Centralized menu and pricing master with per‑outlet overrides (e.g., surcharges for airports).
  - Outlet KPIs: daily sales, avg check, food cost %, labour %, voids/discounts, NPS score.
  - Cross‑outlet comparisons and rankings; drill‑down into a single outlet’s dashboards.
  - Franchise module: royalty calculation (percentage of net sales, minimum guarantee), invoice generation for franchise fees.
- UI:
  - “HQ Console” view restricted to HQ/admin roles.
  - Outlet selector that filters all other reports and widgets.

## 6. Supplier, Procurement & Cost Control

Build a procurement workflow fully linked to inventory and cost control:

- Data:
  - Suppliers: id, name, contact, terms, lead time, rating.
  - Catalog: supplier‑specific items, pack size, pack cost, contracted price, last purchase price.
  - PR (Purchase Request), PO (Purchase Order), GRN (Goods Received Note).
- Features:
  - Generate PRs from low‑stock suggestions / par levels per outlet.
  - Convert approved PRs to POs; mark POs as “Sent”, “Partially Received”, “Closed”.
  - GRN creation against POs: update inventory in correct units and record actual cost; flag price variance against contracted cost.
  - Purchase analytics: spend by supplier, item, and outlet; price trend charts; top variance items.
- Include simple approval logic (e.g., manager vs owner) and logs of who approved what.

## 7. BI Dashboards & Forecasting

Create analytics dashboards and simple forecasting utilities on top of the existing data:

- Dashboards:
  - Operations: sales by day/time, average order value, covers, table turn time, best‑selling dishes, channel mix (dine‑in/takeaway/delivery).
  - Finance: net sales vs discounts vs tax vs voids, food cost %, labour %, gross margin.
  - Marketing: loyalty enrollments, redemptions, campaign uplift.
- Visuals:
  - Use charting (e.g., Chart.js or any light JS chart lib) to show time‑series, pie charts, and bar charts.
- Forecasting (simple rule‑based / moving averages):
  - Next‑week sales forecast per outlet based on the last 8–12 weeks and weekday patterns.
  - Suggested production/inventory based on forecast and recipe consumption.
- Implement reusable data‑access functions so that new dashboards can be added easily.

Extend the existing reservations/tables module into a fully featured floor & waitlist manager:

## 9. Workforce & Labour Cost Module

Extend your staff/scheduling into a labour analytics and control module:

- Data:
  - Shifts: employee, role, outlet, start/end time, scheduled hours, hourly rate.
  - Time punches (optionally mocked): actual clock‑in/clock‑out.
- Features:
  - Labour cost calculation per day/outlet/role (scheduled vs actual).
  - KPIs: labour cost % of sales by hour/day; sales per labour hour; tip distribution (optional).

- UI:
  - “Workforce” dashboard with charts and tables by outlet and period.
